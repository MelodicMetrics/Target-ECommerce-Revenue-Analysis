Dashboard 4: Multi-Item Orders Analysis

Purpose

This dashboard explores the relationships between product variety, seller count, multi-item orders (measured via product-order gaps), and total revenue across categories. It provides actionable insights into how these factors influence performance and highlights opportunities for strategic improvements.

Key Insights

  • Insight 1: There is a positive correlation between Distinct Product Count and Product-Order Gap, suggesting that categories with greater product variety tend to drive more multi-product orders.
  • Insight 2: A similar positive correlation exists between Seller Count and Product-Order Gap, indicating that categories with more sellers experience higher multi-product orders.
  • Insight 3: Product-Order Gap is positively correlated with Total Revenue. Categories with larger product-order gaps contribute more significantly to overall revenue.
  • Insight 4: Categories below the trend line in the first two charts may benefit from strategies such as increasing product variety, adding sellers, or improving seller participation to enhance multi-product orders.
  • Insight 5: Categories above the trend lines in all charts demonstrate strong performance. Analyzing their strategies (e.g., effective bundling, diverse offerings) can provide a blueprint for improving underperforming categories.

Dashboard Features

Visualization Overview

The dashboard consists of three key scatter plots, each providing unique insights into the relationships between variables:

  • Product Variety and Multi-Item Orders: Examines the relationship between Distinct Product Count and Product-Order Gap.
  • Seller Contribution to Multi-Item Orders: Explores the impact of Seller Count on Product-Order Gap.
  • Multi-Item Orders Driving Revenue: Highlights how Product-Order Gap correlates with Total Revenue.
