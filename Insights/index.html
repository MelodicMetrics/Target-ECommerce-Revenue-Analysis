Insights from Revenue Analysis and Exploratory Data Analysis

This page is separated into insights that serve to answer each of my three guiding questions. Insights from my Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) helped inform insights from my Revenue Analysis (RA) for each question.

Yearly Analysis Overview

Purpose

The yearly analysis provided a foundational understanding of the dataset and informed decisions across all guiding questions. While 2016 had minimal data and was excluded from Revenue Analysis (RA), it was included in the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) phase to offer a more holistic perspective.

Key EDA Insights

  • 2016 Contribution:
    • Represented less than 5% of the total orders and revenue, significantly underperforming compared to 2017-2018.
    • Key takeaway: Excluding 2016 ensured focus on trends that better represent customer behavior and revenue generation.
  • Yearly Trends (2017-2018):
    • Total revenue increased by 25% between 2017 and 2018, with order volumes growing in tandem.
    • Top-performing categories: Health & Beauty and Gift Watches led revenue growth during this period.
    • State contributions: States like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro dominated revenue generation, contributing over 50% of total revenue.

Guiding Question 1: Which product categories contribute the most to overall revenue, and which are the most revenue-efficient based on sales volume or order count?

EDA Approach

  • Category Revenue Distribution:
    • Analyzed total revenue and order counts by product category.
    • Drill-through views explored revenue contribution per product value bin, defined by the combined price + freight value of products.
    • Key Insight: Products in the $700+ bin contributed 15% of revenue despite making up just 1.8% of products.
    • Products in the $100–$250 range dominated, contributing 39.02% of revenue with 35.76% of products.
  • Revenue Efficiency (RE):
    • Introduced as a measure of average revenue per product.
    • Categories like Gift Watches and Office Furniture exhibited exceptional RE values, signaling high-value products.
  • Relative Difference %:
    • Measured the gap between RE and AOV to highlight multi-item order potential.
    • Categories like Office Furniture (24.90%) and Signaling & Safety Products (26.55%) stood out as leaders in multi-item orders.

RA Findings

  • Health & Beauty:
    • Total revenue: $1,345,068.93, RE: $148.92.
    • Product line count: 2,333 products, with high order volume (8,276 orders).
    • Average Order Value (AOV): $162.23.
  • Gift Watches:
    • Total revenue: $1,234,696.81, RE: $219.00.
    • Product line count: 1,276 products, strong RE despite lower order volume (5,291 orders).
    • Average Order Value (AOV): $233.36.
    • Weighted Average Revenue per Seller (WAR): $150.23.

Recommendations

  • Health & Beauty: Explore bundling strategies for high-volume but moderate RE categories.
  • Gift Watches: Focus on scaling top-performing sellers to enhance RE and AOV.
  • Office Furniture: Prioritize seller acquisition to capitalize on high RE and strong multi-item order potential.

Guiding Question 2: How does the average order value and profitability vary by standardized product category and seller over time?

EDA Approach

  • Top Sellers by Revenue and Product Count:
    • Analyzed contributions from top-performing sellers within categories like Health & Beauty and Gift Watches.
    • Identified revenue concentration disparities:
      • Gift Watches: Top 5 Sellers contributed 62.11% of revenue with 51 sellers overall.
      • Health & Beauty: Top 5 Sellers contributed 25.71% of revenue from 384 sellers overall.
  • Seller Concentration Disparities:
    • Highlighted performance gaps between highly concentrated categories like Gift Watches and more distributed ones like Health & Beauty.
    • Revealed potential for growth in concentrated categories through strategic seller recruitment.

RA Findings

  • Health & Beauty:
    • Seller Count: 384 sellers.
    • Top 5 Sellers contributed only 18.03% of total products sold and 9.08% of total revenue.
    • Average Order Value (AOV): $162.23.
    • Weighted Average Revenue per Seller (WAR): $123.44.
  • Gift Watches:
    • Seller Count: 51 sellers.
    • Top 5 Sellers contributed 62.11% of total revenue, indicating high seller concentration.
    • Average Order Value (AOV): $233.36.
    • Weighted Average Revenue per Seller (WAR): $150.23.
  • Office Furniture:
    • Seller Count: 33 sellers.
    • Achieved the highest AOV at $269.11, driven by premium product offerings.
    • Weighted Average Revenue per Seller (WAR): $178.89.
  • Year-over-Year Growth:
    • Seller participation increased by 33% from 2017 to 2018, growing from 1,763 to 2,349 sellers.
    • Total revenue rose by 17.22% during the same period, despite a slight decline in AOV ($160.05 to $160.02).

Recommendations

  • Health & Beauty: Encourage top-performing sellers to expand product lines to improve revenue concentration and AOV.
  • Gift Watches: Leverage the strong seller concentration to scale top-performing sellers further.
  • Office Furniture: Invest in seller acquisition to capitalize on premium AOV and strong WAR performance.
  • General:
    • Monitor categories with declining AOV to address pricing pressures and increased competition.
    • Focus seller recruitment efforts on high-RE, low-seller-count categories to unlock growth opportunities.

Guiding Question 3: How does average sales revenue vary across states, and are there any notable regional patterns or correlations?

EDA Approach

  • State Revenue Distribution:
    • Analyzed state-level contributions to total revenue and product counts.
    • Key findings:
      • São Paulo: Contributed 37.36% of total revenue and 41.98% of orders.
      • Rio de Janeiro: Contributed 16.23% of revenue, ranking second overall.
      • Emerging Markets: States like Piauí showed YoY revenue growth of 35.24%.
  • Average Order Value (AOV) by State:
    • Used AOV to assess the relative profitability of each state.
    • High-AOV states like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro consistently outperformed others.

RA Findings

  • São Paulo:
    • Revenue share: 37.36%.
    • Strong multi-item order trends in categories like Office Furniture (37.23% revenue) and Signaling & Safety Products (34.58% revenue).
    • High AOV of $178.22, driven by diverse product offerings and top-selling categories.
  • Rio de Janeiro:
    • Revenue share: 16.23%.
    • Ranked second in total revenue and demonstrated consistent performance across key categories.
    • Moderate AOV of $162.11.
  • Piauí:
    • YoY revenue growth: 35.24%, highlighting its potential as an emerging market.
    • Low total revenue share (3.12%) but improving performance in categories like Health & Beauty.

Recommendations

  • São Paulo: Expand product offerings and focus on high Relative Difference % categories like Office Furniture and Signaling & Safety Products to leverage multi-item order trends.
  • Rio de Janeiro: Optimize marketing efforts to maintain strong revenue contributions and explore bundling opportunities in underperforming categories.
  • Piauí: Invest in seller networks and inventory expansion to capitalize on YoY growth and emerging market potential.
  • General: Leverage AOV insights to identify underperforming states and apply targeted strategies for seller acquisition and product diversification.

Conclusion

This insights page consolidates findings from both Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Revenue Analysis (RA) phases, providing a comprehensive understanding of product categories, seller performance, and regional trends. By integrating insights from the EDA, the RA findings were able to build on data-driven foundations, ensuring a robust and actionable analysis.

Key findings emphasize the importance of focusing on categories with high Revenue Efficiency (RE), such as Gift Watches and Office Furniture, which also exhibited high Average Order Value (AOV) and strong multi-item order potential. Categories like Health & Beauty, while leading in total revenue, present opportunities to enhance efficiency through bundling strategies and targeted seller recruitment.

Regional insights highlighted São Paulo as the dominant revenue contributor, driving multi-item order trends in high Relative Difference % categories like Office Furniture and Signaling & Safety Products. Emerging markets such as Piauí demonstrated strong YoY revenue growth, revealing potential for targeted expansion strategies.

These findings inform key actionable strategies:

  • Targeting high-performing categories for seller acquisition, product expansion, and improved bundling opportunities.
  • Optimizing seller networks in key regions such as São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro to leverage their strengths.
  • Investing in emerging markets like Piauí to capitalize on growth potential and diversify revenue streams.
  • Leveraging insights from multi-item order trends to enhance profitability across top-performing states and categories.

By applying these strategies, the insights gained from this analysis can support data-driven decision-making to optimize performance across product categories, sellers, and regions.
